A Review of Economical, Social, Psychological and Environmental Effects

The harmful effects of air pollution extend beyond respiratory issues. Research has linked it to anxiety, dementia, absenteeism from work, criminal behavior, and numerous other consequences.

A recent comprehensive review highlights that air pollution affects not only people's physical health but also their mental well-being. Conducted by MIT Sloan assistant professor Jackson Lu, the review systematically explores the psychological, economic, and social impacts of air pollution. These findings are published in the April edition of 'Current Opinion in Psychology.'
