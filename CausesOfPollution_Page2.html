Air pollution refers to the emission of gases, human-made particles, and chemicals into the atmosphere at concentrations that pose harm to all living organisms. Researchers studying global warming have identified air pollution as a major contributor to this issue.

How have global carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions evolved over time? This chart illustrates the increase in global emissions from the mid-18th century to the present day. Prior to the Industrial Revolution, emissions were minimal. The growth in emissions remained relatively slow until the mid-20th century, with the world emitting 6 billion tonnes of CO2 in 1950. By 1990, this figure had nearly quadrupled to 22 billion tonnes. Emissions have since continued to rise sharply, reaching over 36 billion tonnes annually. Emissions growth has decelerated in recent years, though they have not yet reached their maximum.
